Biography

I am currently a Ph.D student(expected graduation: May 2022) at Georgia Institute of Technology. I work with Professor Ling Liu in the Distributed Data Intensive Systems Lab (DiSL).

My research interest includes security and trust enhanced machine learning and AI systems, data privacy, big (graph) data mining and analysis, and machine learning with a current focus on deep learning and federated learning. You are welcome to visit my homepage for updated research activities.

Prior to attending at Georgia Tech, I received my Bachelor's degree in Electronics and Information Engineering (B.E.) from Huazhong University of Science and Technology. I worked as an undergradute research assistant in Signal Processing and Information Networking in Communication Lab (SINCLab), Wuhan National Laboratory for Optoelectronics for two years (2015-2017), under the supervision of Professor Pan Zhou.

I lived in Atlanta, GA for one year when I was a teenager, and attended Samuel M. Inman middle school and Henry W. Grady High School. I graduted from Inman with Awards for Achieving Highest Average in Science, Outstanding Achievement in ESOL, CRCT (math & science), and Honor Roll Certificate.

Ongoing Research Projects

1. Adversarial Deep Learning: Attacks and Defenses: Research on characterizing adversarial examples in deep learning and designing attack mitigation strategies.

2. Privacy Preserving Machine Learning: Research on studing privacy intrusion in machine learning models and providing privacy preserving solutions. The idea is to design models that could give accurate results while preserving data privacy, usually with differential privacy and local differential privacy.

3. Machine Learning/Big Data Services: Research on deep learning systems and federated learning systems, including machine learning algorithm and system design, performance measurement (benchmarking) and model optimization(model compression and component design).

4. Data Mining: Research on data mining with representation learning(graph embedding, graph neural networks and distributed data mining) and privacy-preserving data utilization.
