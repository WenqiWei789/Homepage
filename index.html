Biography

I am currently a first-year Ph.D student at Georgia Institute of Technology. I work with Professor Ling Liu in the Distributed Data Intensive Systems Lab (DiSL).

My research interest includes big data analytics and machine learning with a current focus on deep learning, generative adversarial networks, data privacy. You are welcome to visit my home page for updated research activities.

Prior to attending at Georgia Tech, I received my Bachelor's degree in Electronics and Information Engineering (B.E.) from Huazhong University of Science and Technology. I worked as an undergradute research assistant at Signal Processing and Information Networking in Communication Lab (SINCLab), Wuhan National Laboratory for Optoelectronics for two years (2015-2017), under the supervision of Professor Pan Zhou.

I lived in Atlanta, GA for a year when I was a teenager, and attended Samuel M. Inman middle school and Henry W. Grady High School. I graduted from Inman with Awards for Achieving Highest Average in Science, Outstanding Achievement in ESOL, CRCT (math & science), and Honor Roll Certificate.

When I'm not working in the lab, you can find me playing tennis and badminton on the court at the Georgia Tech campus.
